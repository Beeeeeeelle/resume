  <p class="project-case__lede">This dissertation tracks how learners from different linguistic, national, and socioeconomic backgrounds engage with generative AI over time. It brings together surveys, interviews, interaction traces, and performance evidence to understand not just whether AI helps, but how AI-mediated learning trajectories diverge across populations.</p>

  <section class="project-case__section">
    <h2>Data and Analytical Scope</h2>
    <ul class="project-case__list">
      <li>Combines self-report and behavioral evidence rather than relying on a single lens.</li>
      <li>Tracks change over time to distinguish short-term novelty from sustained learning adaptation.</li>
      <li>Centers equity by comparing how AI-mediated learning differs across national and socioeconomic contexts.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="project-case__section">
    <h2>Current Direction</h2>
    <p>The dissertation is moving toward a clearer account of how AI-mediated learning develops across time, and for whom those trajectories are more or less beneficial. It connects process, motivation, and equity rather than treating AI use as a single moment or a single outcome variable.</p>
