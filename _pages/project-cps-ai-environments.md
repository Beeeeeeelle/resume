  <p class="project-case__eyebrow">Industry Collaboration · Intel Labs</p>
  <p class="project-case__lede">This project examines what happens when collaborative problem solving no longer happens only between peers, but between peers and a conversational AI. The work focuses on interaction patterns, instructional design decisions, and the conditions under which AI supports or distorts group learning.</p>

  <div class="project-case__detail-grid">
    <div class="project-case__panel">
      <span class="project-case__panel-label">Project Focus</span>
      <p>The study asks how learners coordinate ideas, distribute roles, and negotiate solutions when an AI becomes part of the collaborative environment. Instead of treating AI as a solo productivity tool, the project looks at how it changes the social organization of learning itself.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="project-case__panel">
      <span class="project-case__panel-label">Why It Matters</span>
      <p>AI can either scaffold better collaboration or quietly replace it. This project is designed to identify which design moves preserve peer reasoning and which ones cause the AI to dominate the problem-solving process.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <section class="project-case__section">
    <h2>Study Design</h2>
    <ul class="project-case__list">
      <li>Industry-academic collaboration combining instructional design analysis with learner interaction evidence.</li>
      <li>Examines AI-mediated collaborative tasks rather than isolated individual prompting behavior.</li>
      <li>Connects process data, observed coordination patterns, and learning outcomes to design implications.</li>
    </ul>
