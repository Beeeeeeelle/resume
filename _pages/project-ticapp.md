  <p class="project-case__eyebrow">Design-Based Research · Mobile Learning</p>
  <p class="project-case__lede">TicApp translates research on Chinese character acquisition into a live mobile learning experience. The project connects empathy-driven UX, cognitive load theory, and iterative field testing to support learners who can speak Chinese but struggle to read, recognize, and type characters efficiently.</p>

      <span class="project-case__panel-label">Design Goal</span>
      <p>The app is designed for learners who often fall through the cracks of conventional Chinese instruction, especially heritage speakers and diverse learners whose spoken fluency is stronger than their character recognition and typing fluency.</p>

  <section class="project-case__section">
    <h2>Project Highlights</h2>
    <ul class="project-case__list">
      <li>Builds around a typing-based learning mechanism that links sound, form, and meaning in one integrated action.</li>
      <li>Uses design-based research to move from practical learner problem to implementation, revision, and documented design principles.</li>
      <li>Applies empathy-driven UX and identity-aware design decisions for populations underserved by standard tools.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>

  <section class="project-case__section">
    <h2>Outcome</h2>
    <p>TicApp became a strong example of how learning science, multilingual learner needs, and app design can be combined into a project that is both research-grounded and visibly implemented. The project also produced a documented design case that makes the design rationale legible to academic audiences.</p>
