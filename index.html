Designing Learning for All

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Purdue University’s Learning Design and Technology program, co-advised by Dr. Victoria Lynn Lowell (Walker) and Dr. Marisa Exter . I earned my master’s degree in Instructional Systems Technology at Indiana University Bloomington , where I was advised by Dr. Curtis J. Bonk . My research focuses on how learners and educators engage with generative AI, particularly in self-directed learning, learner agency, and the design of AI-mediated learning environments. My work combines empirical research with design practice: I develop educational tools, have built my own learning app, and draw on three years of teaching and program experience at Indiana University Bloomington.

My work spans three intersecting strands:

{% include research-venn.html %}

Methodology

I study AI-mediated learning through a combination of synthesis, design, and empirical inquiry, moving between field-level reviews and real-world learning environments.

Systematic Reviews

Map patterns, gaps, and design implications across large bodies of GenAI and language learning research.

Design-Based Research

Build and iteratively refine learning tools in authentic educational settings, including apps and AI-supported environments.

Mixed-Methods Studies

Combine surveys, interviews, interaction traces, and performance data to understand how learning changes over time.

Measurement & Validation

Develop and validate instruments and frameworks for studying self-directed learning, motivation, and AI-integrated processes.
