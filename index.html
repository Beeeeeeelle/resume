I earned my Ph.D. in Learning Design and Technology from Purdue University, where I was co-advised by Dr. Victoria Lynn Lowell (Walker) and Dr. Marisa Exter . I earned my master’s degree in Instructional Systems Technology at Indiana University Bloomington , where I was advised by Dr. Curtis J. Bonk . My research examines how generative AI reshapes learner agency, authenticity, and equity across learning contexts. I build this agenda through learning analytics, mixed-methods inquiry, measurement, and design-based research, drawing on behavioral data and person-centered methods to understand how learners differ in their engagement with AI. My work combines empirical research with design practice: I develop educational tools, translate theory into usable learning designs, and draw on three years of teaching and program experience at Indiana University Bloomington. Recent work has been recognized with the Global Smart Education Innovation Prize for the PA-SDA Scale (2025), the AECT Outstanding Journal Article Award for a collaborative study with Intel Labs on AI-mediated learning (2025), and a World Digital Education Alliance (WDEA) Certificate of Recognition for the PeteGPT AI tutoring project (2026).

我于普渡大学获得学习设计与技术方向博士学位，师从Victoria L. Lowell博士和Marisa Exter博士；此前于印第安纳大学伯明顿分校获得教学系统技术硕士学位，师从Curtis J. Bonk博士。

我的研究关注生成式人工智能如何重塑不同学习情境中的学习者能动性、真实学习体验与教育公平。我综合运用学习分析、混合研究方法、测量研究与基于设计的研究方法，探究学习者在与AI互动过程中的个体差异、学习路径与支持机制。我的工作连接实证研究与设计实践，既关注理论建构与机制解释，也致力于开发教育技术工具，并将研究发现转化为可实施的学习设计方案。这一研究取向也受到我在印第安纳大学三年教学与项目实践经验的持续影响。
