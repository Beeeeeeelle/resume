Design Toward the Future of Learning for ALL

I am a second-year doctoral student in the Department of Learning Design and Technology at Purdue University, co-advised by Dr. Victoria Lynn Lowell (Walker) and Dr. Marisa Exter. I research, integrate, and design instructional methods and technologies including web applications, artificial intelligence, and mixed reality to enhance learners' learning experience in blended and online learning environments. I designed and developed a mobile application, TIC APP, available for download on the App Store. More details can be found here.

I earned my master’s degree in Instructional Systems Technology, Indiana University Bloomington, advised by Dr. Curtis J. Bonk. After graduation, I worked as the product manager at HiLink in Chicago, as an Instructional & Research Manager in the Cyber Lab at IUPUI for a year, and as visiting faculty in the Chinese flagship program at Indiana University for three years, teaching language courses, overseeing and training tutors, and managing the tutoring program.

My recent research focuses on investigating the transformative impact of emerging technologies on language learning and teaching, with particular emphasis on self-directed learning (SDL) and authentic assessment in multilingual contexts. Specifically, I categorize my CALL research into two streams: responsive and inventive CALL.

In responsive CALL, I evaluate and apply emerging technologies in language learning, contributing to the field by bridging critical gaps between technology and language pedagogy and learning strategies. One of my approaches involved studying how YouTube content creators incorporate AI tools in language education, offering insights into real-world applications of these technologies (Project details). Additionally, I had the opportunity to contribute to the field through a systematic review of ChatGPT’s applications in language education, which aims to support future research in this rapidly evolving area.

In inventive CALL, my focus is on creating tools that address real-world needs identified through teaching. I designed and developed a mobile app, TIC APP, that created an authentic yet structured online chat environment to facilitate learners’ holistic processing of phonological visual chunks.
